The Global Missing Migrant crisis

Background

This collection presents a high level overview of migrants who disappeared while aiming for international destinations, gathered by the Missing Migrants Project under the aegis of the International Organization for Migration (IOM) from 2014 onwards. It captures the incidents resulting in fatalities and missing people, displaying the hurdles faced by migrants in transit. This data set stands as a testament to the departed and acknowledges the profound impact on the communities and families left behind.

This visualisation serves as an exploration into these incidents. The data recorded on these incidents is only the tip of the inceberg in regards to this crisis. Numbers of dead and missing people are the minimum numbers affected by the incidents, and there are likely many more people and many more incidents that occured in this time frame.

The map shows that most migration incidents happen when the migrants are coming from Afghanistan, Mexico, Venezuela and Morroco. This is likely due to the heavy volume of migration happening out of these countries, the geopolitical climate of the areas, as well as the harsh environmental conditions surrounding land and water migration.

There were a large number of high volume incidents of drowning in the Central Mediterranean in 2016, surpassing the previous most deadly year of 2015.

It is essential that policy makers address the global missing migrant crisis. To substantially decrease the deaths of migrants during transit, it is crucial to create and strengthen legal pathways. These could incluse such as resettlement opportunities, more visa freedom, and channels for family reunification as safer alternatives to perilous journeys. Enhanced collaboration with transit nations can facilitate humane border controls. Moreover, the establishment of protection mechanisms, like safe havens in transit countries and ensuring access to fair asylum procedures, can provide interim safety and guidance, reducing the likelihood of life-threatening situations for migrants.
